Introduction

Welcome to our website dedicated to promoting gender equality in education. We believe that every individual, regardless of their gender, should have access to quality education. Unfortunately, many girls and women around the world still face significant barriers when it comes to learning opportunities. Our mission is to highlight these inequalities and to advocate for systemic changes that ensure everyone can pursue their education without discrimination. Through data, case studies, and real-life stories, we aim to shed light on the global issue of gender-based disparities in education, while also offering ways to get involved and help make a difference. Together, we can empower girls and women, ensuring that future generations are able to reach their full potential through equal educational opportunities.

Fact 1

According to UNICEF estimates, around the world, 122 million girls are out of school, including 34 million of primary school age, and 87 million of secondary school age. Globally, primary, and secondary school enrollment rates are getting closer to equal for girls and boys (92% male, 90% female).

Fact 2

Girls are more likely to drop out of school than boys in certain regions due to poverty, cultural practices, and safety concerns. This increases gender inequality in society.

Fact 3

Gender equality in education leads to better economic outcomes for communities. Educating girls and women also reduces poverty and promotes healthier families.
